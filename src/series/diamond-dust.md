# Diamond Dust

::: {.flatplan_section .flatplan_layout_left_text}

::: {.flatplan_story_text}
Winter was harshly beautiful and went away slowly and brilliantly.

Born as an accidental offshoot of a larger journey, this series captures a fleeting micro-universe discovered frozen underfoot. An unusually snowy winter transformed the melting forest tracks into a transient laboratory of pure form, crystal structures, and trapped air.

Locked within the ice, fallen autumn leaves and fragments of pine bark became ancient inclusions preserved in amber. Above them, clusters of air bubbles assembled into cosmic constellations, working as natural optical lenses that magnify and distort the underlying textures. Stripped of familiar scale, these macro-fragments document a fragile, temporary alignment of ice, air, and organic life—a silent art gallery that dissolved completely within days.
